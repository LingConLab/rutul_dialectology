Concessive marking

Ivan Netkachev

Last update: 2024-01-10

In most cases, concessive semantics (“Although X, Y”) is expressed by a combination of a conditional converb and an additive particle. However, some dialects allow for other constuctions as well — to convey concessive meaning, additive may attach to non-conditional verbforms. Parataxis (i.e. juxtaposition of two finite clauses) is also a frequent strategy to express this semantics.
