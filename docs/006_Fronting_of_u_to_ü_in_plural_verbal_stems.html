Fronting of u to ü in plural verbal stems

Nikita Beklemishev

Last update: 2023-06-25

<Rutul stems inflect for number in periphrastic verb forms.> Verbal number interacts with animacy , which sometimes allows us to contrast human plural (HPL) and animal/inanimate (NPL) plural forms of the verb. Some verbs with the root vowel /u/ undergo fronting of the stem in plural, i.e. alternation u ~ ü. In Kina, this applies to both HPL and NPL stems. [Danielms] However, the set of verbs accessible to fronting, and whether the fronting happens in NPL, varies across dialects. In this feature, we diagnose this by eliciting two verbs hulqas ‘to play’ and ʁurʁas ‘to stroll’, in prototypical SG, NPL and HPL contexts (in periphrastic past). The sounds in check (preliminary values) are the vowels in the first syllable. In final values, values u / uˁ are grouped together as [-front], and values ü / üˁ are grouped together as [+front].
