Stative verb ‘be’, past tense suffix

Nastya Ivanova

Last update: 2023-06-04

In the short form of the past tense, the stative verbs are marked with the past tense suffix -j in the majority of varieties (e.g. a-j ‘was not there’). In the long form of the past tense, the past tense of the copula is employed, cf. ani jiʔi-j in Kina or ana i-na in Khnov.
