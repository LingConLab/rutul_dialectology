Ergative stem of the medial demonstrative

Author

Maksim Melenchenko

Published

August 28, 2025

This feature shows the specific form of the ergative stem of the medial (ha-) demonstrative. This is the stem used in the ergative form of the demonstrative, followed by the ergative suffix. There are four contexts to distinguish between human / non-human referents and singular / plural referents. The speakers were asked to translate four sentences: ‘He said that’ (human singular), ‘They said that’ (human plural), ‘[The cow died.] She ate bad grass’ (non-human singular), ‘[The cows died.] They ate bad grass’ (non-human plural). For each speaker, first reaction was coded (although the elicitor tried to make sure that this reaction was not an error due to the misunderstanding of the context). Ergative stem for singular human referents is ha-nu- / ha-now- in Ikhrek, Myukhrek, Kina, Luchek and adjacent villages; ha-na- in Shinaz; ha-ni(j) in Rutul and adjacent villages. With singular non-human referents, greater variance is observed: ha-ni- in Ikhrek; ha-ji- in Kina and Luchek; ha-dɨ- in Rutul and adjacent villages; ha-jdi- / ha-jdɨ- in Myukhrek, Shinaz and adjacent villages. In Khnov, the distinction between the singular and the plural form was neutralized in the form ha-na-, which is used in both contexts. Ergative stem for plural human referents is ha-biš- / ha-bɨš- in all villages except Khnov. With plural referents, stem ha-jmɨ- is attested in Myukhrek, Shinaz, Luchek and adjacent villages, while in Ikhrek and the villages close to Rutul, ha-(m)mɨ- is used. In Khnov, the singular and the plural were neutralized in the plural form ha-(m)mɨ-, which is used in both contexts.
